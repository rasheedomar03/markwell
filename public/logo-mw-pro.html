Markwell — MW Monogram Study
8 Professional Concepts
Inspired by LV, Chanel, VW monogram technique — interlocking, negative space, unified forms
01 — The Unified Mark
The Unified Mark
One closed path that reads M across the top and W across the bottom. The same form, rotated 180°. Like a playing card — flip it over and the symbol still reads. Unique, proprietary, impossible to confuse with any other brand.
02 — Interlocked
Interlocked
M and W overlapping — shared strokes at the center. LV, Chanel, Gucci use this exact technique.
03 — Negative Space
Negative Space
M carved into a gold block. The letterform lives in the cutout — the mark is what's removed.
04 — The Coin
The Coin
Unified mark inside a coin ring. Medallion quality — looks great as a wax seal, embroidery patch, or embossed label.
05 — Stack Monogram
Stack Monogram
M over W, sharing outer legs, split by a hairline rule. Classic monogram tradition — timeless.
06 — Diamond Center
Diamond Center
M and W legs converge on a shared diamond. The diamond = the mark, the intersection point.
07 — Geometric Grid
Geometric Grid
M built on a perfect isometric grid — precise triangular geometry. Swiss design approach, very architectural.
08 — Hairline
Hairline Nested
M nested inside a large W outline — thin hairline outer, bold inner. Fashion-brand luxury feel, Chanel-inspired.
